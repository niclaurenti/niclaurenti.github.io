I am Ph.D. student at the University of Milan.
My field of study is theoretical particle physics, that means that I study the fundamental interactions that describe
the physics at a scale smaller than the size of the proton.
In particular I am focused on the strong interaction, i.e. the Quantum Chromodynamics, that is the force that keeps
the atomic nuclei together and that is responsible of bounding the so-called quarks (the proton constituents) into protons
or neutrons.

My research consists into the developement of methods and computational tools that use neural networks and machine learning to
investigate the internal structure of the proton, studying experimental data collected at particle accelerators like
the proton-proton collider [LHC](https://home.cern/science/accelerators/large-hadron-collider) at [CERN](https://home.cern) and the
electon-proton colliders like [HERA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HERA_(particle_accelerator)) at [DESY](https://www.desy.de) or
[SLAC](https://www6.slac.stanford.edu) at Stanford.
